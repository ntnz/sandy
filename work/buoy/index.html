Problem

How might we create a magazine brand within Aotearoa’s market for youth (aged 14-24) to embrace and encourage alternative expression in a world that values trends and social conformity?

Approach

The core of this project was based on interview findings from the target demographic of Aotearoa citizens aged 14 to 24 years old with interests in alternative culture. These findings revealed that self-expression and listening to a wide range of music are regarded as highly important pieces within their lives, which led the magazine to embrace a bold and unconventional visual style to capture the expressive and broad nature of the alternative scene.

Additionally, an integral part of the brand was centred around creating a sense of belonging and community to users through creating a sense of representation, as connection is a basic human need, and alternative-aligning people tend to find themselves alienated within popular culture.
